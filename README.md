# Geo-spatial analysis

# What is the best location in Warkworth for a new bus station when the northern expressway bus service extends further north?
Identifying the most suitable location for a new bus station in Warkworth requires a spatially-informed decision-making process that considers multiple geographic, social, and infrastructural variables.

Bus stops are essential for ensuring reliable and organized public transit, making daily travel more convenient and accessible for everyone. As urban areas expand and regional transport networks evolve, the strategic location of transport infrastructure such as bus stations plays a crucial role in shaping accessibility, mobility, and urban growth. With the planned extension of the Northern Expressway bus service further north, Warkworth, a rapidly growing township north of Auckland, becomes a key node in the regional transit network. Identifying the most suitable location for a new bus station in Warkworth requires a spatially informed decision-making process that considers multiple geographic, social, and infrastructural variables.
In the context of regional towns like Warkworth, where infrastructure is developing in tandem with population growth, the integration of bus services with existing and planned urban features becomes even more critical. According to Currie and Delbosc (2011), stations placed in areas with high walkability, proximity to key services, and seamless road connectivity tend to see higher ridership and community support. This project uses a GIS-based approach to assess land suitability through a structured spatial workflow grounded in academic research and local planning policies.
